Onigiri
from Spirited Away


Description

In the movie, a little girl named Chihiro must work in a mysterious spa for magical spirits
while attempting to find her enchanted parents (they've been transformed into pigs
--who may soon be served for dinner to the magical guests!)

When Chihiro becomes overewhelmed and despairs of ever freeing herself and her parents,
her friend Haku offers her a Japanese rice ball (called "onigiri"). As she eats, she calms down
and renews her resolve to defeat the sorceress and rescue her parents.

Ingredients

  • 6 mini sheets of roasted seaweed (also called "nori")
  • 3/4 cup of shredded, cooked salmon
  • 1 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 6 cups cooked sticky rice
  • 1 triangle shaped cookie cutter (or onigiri mold)

Steps:

  1. Start by slicing your nori sheets in half lengthwise. Cut into 12 strips about 1 1/4" wide.
  2. Mix the mayonnaise into your salmon until well coated. Make sure to break the salmon filet
    into small chunks beforehand. Set aside the salmon mix and nori.
  3. While your rice is slightly warm, not hot, put your cookie cutter or onigiri mold on a flat surface.
    Place an 8" x 8" sheet of plastic wrap over the cookie cutter and push some of it down inside
    so that the inside of the cookie cutter is completely lined with plastic wrap.
  4. Scoop approximately 2 tbsp of rice into the bottom of the cutter and press it flat with a spoon or
    small rubber spatula. Push the rice around to fill in your cutter/mold to make sure the bottom is
    completely filled.
  5. Place 1 tsp salmon filling into the middle of the rice, spreading it around just a little, so there will
    be a little salmon in each bite of rice when you're finished.
  6. Place a couple more tablespoons of rice on top and flatten out like before. Wrap the plastic tightly
    over the exposed end of the onigiri and set it aside.
  7. Repeat with the remaining rice and salmon until you run out of ingredients. Let the onigiri
    set for 5-10 minutes.
  8. When ready to serve, remove the plastic wrap and press a nori strip around the bottom of
    each onigiri. The rice should still be sticky enough that the nori adheres with no problems.
  9. Serve at a spa for magical creatures while brainstorming ideas to save your loved ones!
